How to Make Orange Chicken


Orange chicken is a popular Chinese-American dish featuring crispy, golden-brown chicken pieces tossed in a tangy, sweet orange sauce. To make it, bite-sized pieces of chicken are coated in a seasoned batter and deep-fried until crispy. Meanwhile, a sauce is prepared by simmering orange juice, zest, soy sauce, sugar, vinegar, garlic, ginger, and a touch of cornstarch to thicken it. Once the chicken is fried, it's tossed in the sauce until each piece is coated, creating a delicious balance of sweet, sour, and savory flavors. The dish is often garnished with green onions and served over steamed rice, making it a satisfying and flavorful meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup orange marmalade
  • 1/2 cup Kansas City-style BBQ sauce
  • 1/4 cup low sodium soy sauce
  • 1 (1 pound) bag frozen fully cooked chicken nuggets
  • sliced green onions (optional)
  • sesame seeds (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C). Place frozen nuggets in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  2. Bake in the preheated oven until hot and crispy, 11 to 13 minutes, or according to package directions.
  3. Meanwhile, whisk marmalade, BBQ sauce, and soy sauce together in a small saucepan and heat over low heat until hot, about 5 minutes.
  4. Layer the lasagna according to the recipe instructions.
  5. Place nuggets in a large bowl. Drizzle sauce over the top. Toss to coat.
